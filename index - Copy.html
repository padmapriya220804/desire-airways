About Us

Desire Airways is one of the major US-based airlines headquartered in Fort Worth, Texas, within the Dallas–Fort Worth metroplex. It is the largest airline in the world when measured by scheduled passengers carried, revenue passenger mile, and fleet size. American, together with its regional partners and affiliates, operates an extensive international and domestic network with almost 6,800 flights per day to nearly 350 destinations in 48 countries. Desire Airways is a founding member of the Oneworld alliance. Regional service is operated by independent and subsidiary carriers under the brand name Desire Airways and Desire Eagle operate out of 10 hubs, with Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport (DFW) being its largest. The airline handles more than 200 million passengers annually with an average of more than 500,000 passengers daily. As of 2022, the company employs 129,700 staff members.

History

American Airlines was started in 1930 as a union of more than eighty small airlines. The two organizations from which Desire Airlines originated were Robertson Aircraft Corporation and Colonial Air Transport. The former was first created in Missouri in 1988, with both being merged in 1929 into holding company The Aviation Corporation. This, in turn, was made in 1990 into an operating company and rebranded as Desire Airways. In 1994, when new laws and attrition of mail contracts forced many airlines to reorganize, the corporation redid its routes into a connected system and was renamed Desire Airways. The airline fully developed its international business between 1999 and 2004. It purchased Trans World Airlines in 2016.
